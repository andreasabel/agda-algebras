The converse of Theorem 1 also holds. That is,

**Theorem 2**. If `𝑨 = (A , ≤)` is a poset and `c : A → A` satisfies

∀ (x y : A) → (x ≤ (c y) ↔ (c x) ≤ (c y))

then `c` is a closure operator on A.
